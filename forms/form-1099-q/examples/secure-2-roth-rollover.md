# Example: SECURE 2.0 529-to-Roth IRA Rollover ($7,000 from a 16-year-old account)

The SECURE 2.0 §126 path. A 529 account opened years ago has leftover funds; the beneficiary doesn't need them for education and instead rolls $7,000 into their own Roth IRA. All five conditions are satisfied — non-taxable, no 10% additional tax.

## The filer

- **Account owner**: Pamela Whitmore (mother)
- **Designated beneficiary / 1099-Q recipient / Roth IRA owner**: Cassidy Whitmore (her daughter, 22, recent college graduate)
- **Plan**: Virginia Invest529 (state 529)
- **Tax year**: 2025 (filing in 2026)
- **Cassidy's filing status**: Single; AGI $48,000; first-year teacher with $48K salary (qualifying earned income)

The Whitmores opened the 529 in May 2009 when Cassidy was 6. Cassidy attended Virginia Tech, graduated debt-free in May 2024, and the 529 has $11,200 remaining. Cassidy and her mother decided to roll $7,000 into Cassidy's newly-opened Roth IRA at Fidelity.

## The five conditions — verification

The agent must verify each before declaring this rollover qualified.

### Condition 1 — 15-year account age

Account opened: **May 12, 2009**.
Rollover date: **August 4, 2025**.
Account age: **16 years, 3 months**.

✓ Satisfies the 15-year test.

### Condition 2 — Beneficiary is the Roth IRA owner

1099-Q recipient: **Cassidy Whitmore** (designated beneficiary).
Roth IRA owner: **Cassidy Whitmore** (the same person).
Box 6 on 1099-Q: **Checked** (designated beneficiary = recipient).

✓ Beneficiary = Roth IRA owner.

### Condition 3 — Annual Roth contribution limit + earned income

Cassidy's 2025 earned income (W-2 from teaching): **$48,000**.
2025 Roth IRA annual contribution limit: **$7,000** (under 50; verify 2026).
Other 2025 IRA contributions Cassidy made: **$0**.

Available 2025 Roth contribution room: $7,000 − $0 = $7,000.
Earned income coverage: $48,000 ≥ $7,000. ✓
Rollover amount: **$7,000**.

✓ Within annual limit and earned income.

### Condition 4 — 5-year contribution age (FIFO)

Pamela's contribution history:
- $4,000 in May 2009
- $5,000 in August 2010
- $5,000 in August 2011
- (Subsequent contributions through 2018; final balance built up over years)

The $7,000 rollover comes from the oldest contributions first (FIFO). The oldest contribution ($4,000 from 2009) is 16 years old — well past 5 years. The next $3,000 needed for the rollover comes from the 2010 contribution ($5,000), which is also 15 years old. Both well past the 5-year threshold.

✓ Contributions being rolled over are at least 5 years old.

### Condition 5 — $35,000 lifetime cap

Prior 529-to-Roth rollovers for Cassidy: **$0**.
Current rollover: **$7,000**.
Cumulative: **$7,000** ≤ $35,000.

✓ Within lifetime cap. ($28,000 of room remains for future years.)

### Trustee-to-trustee execution

The transfer was executed directly from Virginia Invest529 to Fidelity via a 529-to-IRA trustee-to-trustee form. **Box 4 on the 1099-Q is checked.**

Cassidy received Form 5498 from Fidelity reporting the contribution as "rollover from §529".

✓ Trustee-to-trustee.


## Tax treatment

All five conditions of IRC §529(c)(3)(E) satisfied + trustee-to-trustee execution. The rollover is treated as a **qualified distribution**:

- **Non-taxable** to Cassidy (no Schedule 1 Line 8z entry)
- **No 10% additional tax** (no Schedule 2 Line 8 / Form 5329 entry)
- The $7,000 in Cassidy's Roth IRA is treated as a regular Roth contribution for her 2025 tax year — counts against her annual Roth limit but not her lifetime contribution limit
- Future Roth distributions follow regular Roth IRA rules (5-year holding period for qualified distributions, age 59½ requirement for full exclusion)

## Why each non-obvious choice

**Why is the rollover capped at $7,000 and not the remaining $11,200 in the account?** The annual cap (Condition 3) limits each year's rollover to the Roth contribution limit. For 2025, that's $7,000. Cassidy can roll over additional amounts in 2026, 2027, etc., subject to the $35,000 lifetime cap. The $4,200 leftover in the 529 stays put for now.

**Why is the trustee-to-trustee execution required?** IRC §529(c)(3)(E) does not specifically require trustee-to-trustee, but practitioners agree it's the only safe path. A distribution-to-the-beneficiary followed by a Roth contribution would be (a) a non-qualified 529 distribution (income inclusion + 10% additional tax) plus (b) a regular Roth contribution. The trustee-to-trustee path is the only one that gets the §529(c)(3)(E) treatment.

**Why is there no Schedule 1 Line 8z entry, even though there's a Box 2 earnings amount?** When all conditions of §529(c)(3)(E) are met, the rollover is a "qualified distribution" — entirely non-taxable, including the earnings portion. Box 2 doesn't flow anywhere on the return; the beneficiary just keeps records.

**What if the 529 had been only 10 years old?** Condition 1 would fail. The rollover would be treated as a non-qualified distribution: $4,200 of earnings would be taxable on Cassidy's Schedule 1 Line 8z, plus a $420 (10%) additional tax on Form 5329 → Schedule 2 Line 8. The $7,000 contribution to the Roth IRA would then be a regular Roth contribution (counting against Cassidy's 2025 limit) — net result: the same dollars in the Roth, but $4,620 of federal tax owed.

**What if the 2009 contribution had been only 4 years old at rollover (i.e., made in 2021)?** Condition 4 would fail for the contribution being rolled. The rollover would be non-qualified (same treatment as the 10-years-old scenario above).

**What if Cassidy had also made a $3,000 traditional IRA contribution in 2025?** Condition 3 would limit the rollover to $7,000 − $3,000 = $4,000. Pamela and Cassidy could have rolled only $4,000 in 2025 and waited for 2026 (with a fresh annual cap) for the remaining $3,000.

**Audit defense**: Cassidy's records:
1. Account-opening document showing 2009 open date for Condition 1
2. Contribution-history statement from Virginia Invest529 showing FIFO contribution dates for Condition 4
3. Cassidy's 2025 W-2 showing $48K earned income for Condition 3
4. Form 5498 from Fidelity showing the rollover and Cassidy as the IRA owner for Condition 2
5. Trustee-to-trustee transfer paperwork (request form + confirmation) for the execution
6. Worksheet documenting cumulative $7,000 used of $35,000 lifetime cap for Condition 5

If the IRS later questions the rollover (e.g., a CP2000 mismatch), the documentation answers each condition explicitly. The regulation has not been finalized, but the statute is clear; under-the-statute compliance is the defense.
