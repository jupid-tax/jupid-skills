# Common Form 568 Mistakes

The audit-trip and notice-generating mistakes most commonly seen on California Form 568. Each entry includes the trigger, the consequence, and the citation.

---

## 1. Not filing Form 568 because the LLC had $0 revenue

**Trigger**: First-year LLC owner thinks "no revenue = no tax = no return".

**Reality**: Every California LLC owes $800 (R&TC §17941) and must file Form 568 every year, regardless of revenue or profit, until properly dissolved with the Secretary of State (Forms LLC-3 + LLC-4/7).

**Consequence**: Failure-to-file penalty under R&TC §19131 — $18 per month per partner/member, max 12 months ($216 per member per year). For a 2-member LLC this is $432/year on top of the $800 owed.

**Citation**: R&TC §17941, R&TC §19131.

---

## 2. Relying on the AB 85 first-year exemption after 2023

**Trigger**: User read online that the $800 first-year tax is waived; user formed LLC in 2024 or later.

**Reality**: AB 85 (Stats. 2020, ch. 8) waived the first-year $800 tax for LLCs formed in **tax years 2021, 2022, and 2023 only**. The provision was not extended. LLCs formed in 2024 and later owe $800 in their first year, due by the 15th day of the 4th month after formation.

**Consequence**: Late-payment penalty + interest on the $800 from the original due date.

**Citation**: AB 85 (Stats. 2020, ch. 8); FTB Limited Liability Company page <https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/business/types/limited-liability-company/index.html>.

---

## 3. Using federal gross receipts on Schedule IW Line 1

**Trigger**: User computes Schedule IW (Total Income) by copying federal Form 1065 Line 1c.

**Reality**: Schedule IW asks for **gross receipts plus COGS** — i.e., gross revenue, not gross profit. For a reseller with $480K revenue and $300K COGS, federal gross income is $180K but Schedule IW Total Income is $480K. This determines the LLC fee tier.

**Consequence**: Underreporting Total Income → underpaid LLC fee → 10% underpayment penalty + interest.

**Citation**: R&TC §17942(b).

---

## 4. Underestimating Form 3536 in a growth year

**Trigger**: LLC paid $900 estimated fee in June (matched prior year) but actual year-end fee turns out to be $2,500.

**Reality**: §17942(d) imposes a 10% penalty on any underpayment by more than 10%.

**Consequence**: $160 penalty on a $1,600 underestimate. (For larger jumps — e.g., $0 estimated, $11,790 actual — penalty is $1,179.)

**Mitigation**: When growth is uncertain, estimate at the next-higher tier. Overpayments flow back via Form 568 Line 8 against actual fee.

**Citation**: R&TC §17942(d).

---

## 5. Forgetting to dissolve with SOS after "Final Return"

**Trigger**: LLC owner files final Form 568 marking "Final Return", then assumes the LLC is closed.

**Reality**: Marking "Final Return" on Form 568 tells the FTB. It does **not** tell the SOS. Until Form LLC-3 (Dissolution) and Form LLC-4/7 (Cancellation) are filed with the SOS, the entity remains active and accrues another $800 next year.

**Consequence**: Continuing $800 + filing requirement until SOS dissolution is complete.

**Citation**: California Corporations Code §17707.01-§17707.09 (LLC dissolution and cancellation).

---

## 6. Not collecting Form 3832 for a nonresident member

**Trigger**: Multi-member LLC has one member who lives in Nevada; LLC files Form 568 without Form 3832 and without Form 592 withholding.

**Reality**: Nonresident members must either sign Form 3832 (consent to California jurisdiction) **or** the LLC must withhold 7% of California-source distributable income > $1,500 and complete Schedule T.

**Consequence**: FTB notice; LLC assessed retroactive withholding + Schedule T tax + interest.

**Citation**: R&TC §18662(b), R&TC §17935.

---

## 7. Claiming federal §168(k) bonus depreciation on California

**Trigger**: LLC bought $50K equipment, took 100% federal bonus depreciation, copied the same $50K deduction to California Schedule K.

**Reality**: California does not conform to §168(k). Add-back required; California depreciates under MACRS (e.g., 20% Year 1 on a 5-year asset = $10K).

**Consequence**: Underreported California taxable income; eventual FTB notice with 10-20 year lookback potential.

**Citation**: R&TC §17024, §17250 (depreciation conformity).

---

## 8. Claiming federal §179 over $25K on California

**Trigger**: LLC took $100K federal §179; assumes California allows the same.

**Reality**: California §179 limit is $25,000 (R&TC §17255); excess depreciated under MACRS.

**Consequence**: Same as §168(k) — add-back, underreporting, eventual notice.

**Citation**: R&TC §17255, §24356.

---

## 9. Filing on wrong due date

**Trigger**: Multi-member LLC files Form 568 by April 15 thinking it follows the SMLLC deadline.

**Reality**: Multi-member (partnership-classified) Form 568 is due **March 15**. SMLLC Form 568 is due April 15.

**Consequence**: Late filing penalty under R&TC §19131 ($18/member/month) + late payment penalty if balance owed.

**Mitigation**: California grants automatic 7-month extension to FILE without any form, but the $800, fee, and withholding are still due by the original deadline. Use Form 3537 to pay during extension.

**Citation**: R&TC §18601, §18606.

---

## 10. Filing Form 568 when LLC elected S-corp federally

**Trigger**: LLC filed federal Form 2553 to elect S-corp status; then files Form 568 by habit.

**Reality**: California automatically conforms to federal S-elections (R&TC §23800(a)). The LLC should file **Form 100S**, not Form 568. The $17942 LLC fee does NOT apply; the §23802 1.5% S-corp tax does.

**Consequence**: Wrong return; FTB may correspond to reconcile. Best practice: file an amended return on Form 100S as soon as caught.

**Citation**: R&TC §23800(a), §23802.

---

## 11. Treating an out-of-state LLC with California members as having no nexus

**Trigger**: Wyoming LLC with one California-resident member and online customers nationwide.

**Reality**: Test 1 (active engagement in California) and Test 2 (commercial domicile) are likely met if the California member operates the LLC from California. *Swart Enterprises* (2017) limits FTB's reach for passive 0.2% members but anything close to active involvement triggers the obligation.

**Consequence**: Unfiled Form 568 + unpaid $800/year + late penalties on every year of nexus.

**Mitigation**: When in doubt, consult a CPA or treat the LLC as having California nexus and file. The cost of compliance ($800 + maybe a small fee) is much less than back-taxes after a notice.

**Citation**: R&TC §23101(b), *Swart Enterprises v. Franchise Tax Board* (2017).

---

## 12. Forgetting Form LLC-3 / LLC-4/7 in dissolution

(Same as #5; included again because it's the single most common multi-year mistake — owner thinks they closed the LLC, gets a $800 bill 14 months later because SOS still has it active.)

**Mitigation**: After filing the final Form 568, check SOS BizFile system to confirm cancellation/dissolution forms are recorded. Status should change from "Active" to "Cancelled" or "Dissolved".

**Citation**: California Corporations Code §17707.01.

---

## Quick reminder list for the agent

Before declaring a Form 568 draft ready, walk through this list:

1. ☐ Has the user paid Form 3522 ($800) for this year?
2. ☐ If expected fee > $0, has the user paid Form 3536?
3. ☐ Schedule IW computed using gross-receipts-plus-COGS, not gross profit?
4. ☐ Federal §168(k) and §179-over-$25K added back?
5. ☐ Every nonresident member has either Form 3832 or Form 592-B?
6. ☐ If "Final Return", has user filed SOS Forms LLC-3 / LLC-4/7?
7. ☐ Right deadline (March 15 multi-member, April 15 SMLLC)?
8. ☐ Correct filing entity (Form 568 vs. Form 100S vs. Form 100)?
9. ☐ Multi-state? Schedule R apportionment attached?
10. ☐ Schedule L required (receipts ≥ $250K or assets ≥ $1M)?

If any answer is "no" or "I don't know", surface the issue to the user before finalizing.
