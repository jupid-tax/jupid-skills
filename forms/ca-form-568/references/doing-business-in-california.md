# Doing Business in California (R&TC §23101)

A California LLC must file Form 568 (and pay the $800 + LLC fee) if it is **organized in California** OR if it is **doing business in California** under R&TC §23101. This second prong catches out-of-state LLCs that have California nexus without registering with the Secretary of State.

This is the most-litigated question in California LLC tax. The agent should walk through the tests carefully — getting it wrong creates either unnecessary $800 payments or unfiled-return penalties.

---

## The four tests (R&TC §23101(b))

An LLC is "doing business in California" if **any** of these is true:

### Test 1 — Active engagement in California
The LLC is **actively engaging in any transaction** for the purpose of financial or pecuniary gain or profit in California. This is fact-specific; even one transaction can qualify.

### Test 2 — Organized or commercially domiciled in California
The LLC is organized under California law (registered with California SOS) **or** has its commercial domicile (principal place of business) in California.

### Test 3 — California sales exceed threshold
California sales **exceed** the lesser of $711,538 OR 25% of total sales (per R&TC §23101(b)(2), 2024 indexed value; verify current threshold each year — FTB updates per §23101(d)).

### Test 4 — California real or tangible property exceeds threshold
California real or tangible personal property **exceeds** the lesser of $71,154 OR 25% of total real and tangible personal property (2024 indexed value).

### Test 5 — California compensation exceeds threshold
California compensation paid **exceeds** the lesser of $71,154 OR 25% of total compensation paid (2024 indexed value).

> **Threshold values**: the dollar thresholds in tests 3-5 are inflation-indexed annually per §23101(d). 2024 values shown above. Verify current values at <https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/business/types/limited-liability-company/index.html> or via Pub 1067 each year.

If **any one** test is met, the LLC is "doing business in California" and must file Form 568.

---

## Common gotchas

### Gotcha 1 — Out-of-state LLC with a California member

If the LLC has even one California-resident member and engages in business activity, it likely meets Test 1. The California member's share of LLC activity is treated as California-source income to the member, and the LLC may be deemed to be doing business in California.

The FTB has been aggressive on this — see *Swart Enterprises v. Franchise Tax Board* (2017, Cal. Ct. App.) which limited FTB's reach for passive 0.2% members, but anything close to active or majority is in scope.

### Gotcha 2 — Online business with California customers

An LLC with no physical California presence but enough California customers can meet Test 3 (sales threshold). Post-*Wayfair* (2018), states have aggressively asserted economic-nexus standards even before *Swart*. Even an LLC operating from another state with $711K+ of California sales is probably "doing business in California".

### Gotcha 3 — Single-member LLC owned by a California-resident individual

The owner's California residency does not automatically put the LLC in California — but if the owner conducts the business from California (laptop in San Francisco, customers anywhere), Test 1 (active engagement in California) and Test 2 (commercial domicile) are likely met. The LLC owes $800 and Form 568.

### Gotcha 4 — Real estate LLC holding California property

Even a passive holding LLC owning California real estate generally meets Test 1 and Test 4 — both income from CA real estate (rents) and the property itself. Single-member LLCs holding California rental properties are an extremely common Form 568 filer.

### Gotcha 5 — LLC dissolved mid-year but had California activity earlier

The LLC is "doing business in California" for the part of the year it had nexus, even if it dissolved before year-end. Form 568 is still required (mark "Final Return"), and the $800 is still owed for that tax year. Dissolution with the SOS (Forms LLC-3 + LLC-4/7) is required to stop the $800 from accruing in **future** years.

---

## When the LLC does NOT need to file Form 568

The LLC has **no California nexus** if all of these are true:

- Not organized under California law
- Not registered with California SOS as a foreign LLC doing business in California
- No California-resident members (or only passive small-stake California members under *Swart*)
- No California property (real or tangible)
- No California sales above the threshold
- No California compensation above the threshold
- No California-source business activity

If all five fail (i.e., no nexus on any test), no Form 568 is required and the $800 is not owed.

---

## Voluntary registration

An LLC that has **registered with California SOS as a foreign LLC** ("doing business" registration via Form LLC-5) is automatically required to file Form 568 and pay $800 — even if its actual nexus is borderline. The act of registration creates the obligation.

If the user registered "just in case" but never actually did business in California, advise them to **withdraw the registration** (Form LLC-4/7) for any year they didn't have actual business activity. Otherwise the $800 keeps accruing.

---

## "Final Return" mechanics

When the LLC ceases doing business in California:

1. **Mark "Final Return"** on Form 568 Side 1
2. **File the final Form 568** for the partial-year period
3. **File Form LLC-4/7** (Certificate of Cancellation) with California SOS
4. **File Form LLC-3** (Certificate of Dissolution) if a domestic CA LLC

If only step 1-2 happen but not 3-4, the LLC remains "active" with SOS and accrues another $800 next year. Coordination with the SOS filing is critical.
