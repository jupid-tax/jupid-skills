# Form 5498 — Common Mistakes

The 10 most common mistakes filers make when handling Form 5498. Most
result from confusion about what the form is, what it isn't, and how it
relates to the tax return.

---

## 1. Trying to file Form 5498 with the tax return

**The mistake**: User receives Form 5498 in May, panics, and asks how to
file it with their return.

**Why it's wrong**: Form 5498 is filed by the **custodian**, not the
user. The user's copy is for records only. The IRS already has it.

**Fix**: Keep the 5498 in tax records. No filing action.

**Citation**: Form 5498 Instructions — "Recipient's Statement"; IRC
§6047(d).

---

## 2. Thinking Box 1 is the deduction amount

**The mistake**: User sees Box 1 = $7,000 and assumes that's their tax
deduction. But Box 1 is the *contribution* amount, not the *deduction*.

**Why it's wrong**: For traditional IRAs, the deduction depends on
whether the user (or spouse) is covered by an employer retirement plan
and the user's MAGI. A high-earner covered by a 401(k) may have $7,000
in Box 1 but a $0 deduction.

**Fix**: Determine deductibility based on coverage status and MAGI
phaseouts. If fully nondeductible, the contribution is still made but
goes on Form 8606 as basis (not Schedule 1).

**Citation**: IRC §219(g) — phaseout for active participants; Notice
2024-80 — 2025 phaseout amounts.

---

## 3. Filing an amended return when Form 5498 just hadn't arrived yet

**The mistake**: User filed in March, claimed $7,000 IRA deduction. In
May the 5498 arrives showing Box 1 = $7,000. User panics that the IRS
will see a "discrepancy" and amends the return.

**Why it's wrong**: There's no discrepancy. The 5498 confirms what was
claimed. The 5498 is *intentionally* issued after the filing deadline
because contributions can be made up to April 15.

**Fix**: No amendment needed. File the 5498 in tax records.

**Citation**: Form 5498 issuance deadline May 31 (Form 5498 Instructions).

---

## 4. Thinking Box 11 (RMD) means the RMD has been taken

**The mistake**: User sees Box 11 ✓ on their 5498 and concludes their
RMD is satisfied.

**Why it's wrong**: Box 11 means an RMD is *required* for the year
following the 5498 year. It does NOT mean an RMD has been distributed.
The actual RMD is reported on a 1099-R when distributed.

**Fix**: Cross-check whether the user actually took the RMD via the
1099-R or year-end statement. If not, the user must take it before the
deadline (December 31 of the year, or April 1 for first-year RMDs).

**Citation**: Form 5498 Instructions — Box 11 description.

---

## 5. Confusing Box 3 (conversion received) with Form 1040 Line 4b (taxable conversion)

**The mistake**: User has $25,000 in Box 3 (Roth conversion) and reports
$25,000 on Form 1040 Line 4b (taxable amount), failing to apply Form
8606 basis pro-rata.

**Why it's wrong**: Box 3 is the gross conversion. If the user has
nondeductible basis from prior years (tracked on Form 8606), the
*taxable* portion of the conversion is less than the gross. The
pro-rata rule requires Form 8606 Part II to compute the taxable amount.

**Fix**: Complete Form 8606 Part II. Apply pro-rata across all
traditional / SEP / SIMPLE IRAs. Report taxable portion only on Line
4b.

**Citation**: Form 8606 Instructions; IRC §72(e)(8).

---

## 6. Treating Box 5 (FMV) as the only retirement account balance

**The mistake**: User uses Box 5 from a single 5498 to compute next
year's RMD. But the user has IRAs at three custodians.

**Why it's wrong**: For RMD purposes, traditional / SEP / SIMPLE IRAs
aggregate. The denominator is the *aggregate* FMV (sum of Box 5 across
all relevant 5498s).

**Fix**: Sum Box 5 from every traditional / SEP / SIMPLE IRA 5498. Use
the aggregate.

For 401(k)s and other qualified plans, RMDs do *not* aggregate with IRAs
or with each other; each plan's RMD is separate.

**Citation**: IRC §401(a)(9); IRS Pub 590-B.

---

## 7. Missing that a contribution was for the prior year

**The mistake**: User makes a $7,000 IRA contribution on March 5, 2026.
They forget to designate it for tax year 2025 (their intent). The
custodian defaults to 2026. The 2025 5498 shows Box 1 = $0; the 2026
5498 (received May 2027) shows Box 1 = $7,000. User claims the $7,000
deduction on their 2025 return based on intent.

**Why it's wrong**: The IRS sees the 2025 5498 (Box 1 = $0) and the
deduction claim on the 2025 return ($7,000). CP2000 notice incoming.

**Fix**: Contact custodian immediately to re-designate the contribution
to 2025. If custodian agrees, they issue corrected 5498s. If not, amend
the 2025 return to remove the deduction (and potentially claim it on
the 2026 return).

**Citation**: Custodian-specific contribution-designation rules; IRC
§219(f)(3) for the deadline.

---

## 8. Ignoring a 5498 because "I didn't make any contributions this year"

**The mistake**: User receives a 5498 but knows they didn't contribute,
so they discard it.

**Why it's wrong**: Form 5498 is issued for any IRA with year-end activity
or balance, not just contributions. Reasons it might arrive:
- Box 5 (FMV) reporting — issued every year there's a balance
- Box 11 (RMD required) — flagged for the year

If Box 11 is checked, the user has an RMD requirement and needs to act.
Discarding the 5498 means missing that flag.

**Fix**: Read every 5498 received, even when no contribution was made.
File in records.

**Citation**: Form 5498 Instructions — "When to file."

---

## 9. Thinking Box 10 and Box 1 are interchangeable

**The mistake**: User contributed to a Roth IRA. The 5498 shows Box 7 =
"Roth IRA" but Box 1 (not Box 10) is populated. User assumes "Box 1 is
my contribution, regardless of account type."

**Why it's wrong**: Box 1 is for traditional IRA contributions. Box 10
is for Roth contributions. If a Roth account's 5498 has Box 1
populated, the custodian made a coding error.

The error may have downstream effects: the IRS may believe the user
contributed to a *traditional* IRA and look for a corresponding
deduction (or basis on Form 8606). Either way, the records are wrong.

**Fix**: Request corrected 5498 from the custodian.

**Citation**: Form 5498 Instructions — Box 1 vs. Box 10 distinctions.

---

## 10. Trusting Box 12b RMD amount without verifying

**The mistake**: User sees Box 12b = $9,200 and takes $9,200 as their
RMD, satisfied. They have IRAs at three other custodians but figure
this one's 12b is good enough.

**Why it's wrong**: Box 12b is the custodian's computation for the
*single* IRA at that custodian. The user's actual RMD is the sum across
all aggregating IRAs.

If the user takes only $9,200 but the aggregate RMD across all four
IRAs is $14,500, they have a $5,300 shortfall — Form 5329 Part IX
territory.

**Fix**: Compute aggregate RMD using aggregate FMV (sum of Box 5
across all 5498s) ÷ distribution period factor. Take at least the
aggregate from any combination of IRAs.

**Citation**: IRS Pub 590-B — IRA RMD aggregation rules; Form 5498
Instructions — Box 12b limitation.

---

## Bonus pitfall — Roth IRA Box 11 anomaly

If Box 11 is checked on a Roth IRA's 5498 (and the account is owned by
the original owner, not inherited), the custodian made an error. Roth
IRAs have **no lifetime RMD** for the original owner under IRC
§408A(c)(5).

The error doesn't itself create a tax obligation — there's no actual
RMD requirement. But it can cause confusion and the custodian's
automated RMD systems may attempt to distribute amounts.

Fix: Request corrected 5498 from the custodian. Confirm with the
custodian's RMD desk that the account is correctly flagged as Roth
(no RMD).

---

## How to spot 5498 issues proactively

When reviewing a user's 5498, run through this checklist:

1. **Account type (Box 7) matches the populated contribution box?**
   - IRA → Box 1 populated
   - Roth IRA → Box 10 populated
   - SEP → Box 8 populated
   - SIMPLE → Box 9 populated

2. **Total contributions across all 5498s ≤ annual limit?**
   - 2025 IRA limit: $7,000 / $8,000 age 50+
   - 2025 SEP limit: lesser of $70,000 or 25% compensation
   - 2025 SIMPLE deferral: $16,500 / $20,000 age 50+

3. **Roth contributions consistent with MAGI eligibility?**
   - Single 2025 phaseout: $150K-$165K
   - MFJ 2025 phaseout: $236K-$246K
   - MFS 2025 phaseout: $0-$10K

4. **Box 2 rollover matches a corresponding 1099-R?**
5. **Box 3 conversion matches a 1099-R AND Form 8606 Part II?**
6. **Box 4 recharacterization round-trips between two 5498s?**
7. **Box 11 RMD flag consistent with user's age and account type?**
8. **Box 5 FMV matches the user's December 31 statement?**
9. **Multiple custodians: have all 5498s been collected?**
10. **Any contribution-year designation issues?**

Resolve every issue before filing the next year's return — issues
compound when ignored.
