# Example — Solo 401(k) crossing $250K threshold; first-time 5500-EZ filing

## Scenario

**Filer**: Maria Chen, sole proprietor running a consulting LLC (single-member, disregarded for federal tax)

**Plan**: "Maria Chen Consulting LLC Solo 401(k)" — established January 2022, calendar plan year

**Filing year**: Plan year 2025 (January 1, 2025 – December 31, 2025)

**Why this matters**: Maria's solo 401(k) had assets below $250,000 at year-end 2022, 2023, and 2024 — no 5500-EZ filing was required. During 2025, the plan crossed $250,000 for the first time. This triggers the IRC §6058(a) reporting requirement and her first-ever Form 5500-EZ.

## Variant selection

Walked the decision tree:

1. SEP-IRA / SIMPLE IRA / governmental / church plan? No — qualified solo 401(k).
2. One-participant plan? Yes (owner only, no spouse, no employees).
3. Year-end assets ≥ $250,000? **Yes — $287,850.**
4. → File **Form 5500-EZ**.

This is Maria's first-ever 5500-EZ filing. Prior years (2022, 2023, 2024) were below $250,000 and NOT required. The plan effective date is 1/1/2022 but reporting starts only with the year that the threshold is crossed.

## Filing channel

Maria has two options:

**Option A — Paper to IRS Ogden**: Print, sign in ink, mail Certified with Return Receipt to:

```
Department of the Treasury
Internal Revenue Service
Ogden, UT 84201-0027
```

Postmark by 7/31/2026 (last day of 7th month after plan year end).

**Option B — Electronic via EFAST2**: Available since plan year 2020 for 5500-EZ. Maria would need to obtain EFAST2 filer + signer credentials (signer credentials require postal-mail PIN, allow 7–14 days). Given this is a first-time filing and Maria expects to file annually going forward, electronic may be worth the setup overhead.

**Recommendation**: Maria chooses paper for first-year simplicity. She'll evaluate EFAST2 for plan year 2026 once she has time to set up credentials.
