# Example — 50-employee 401(k); 5500-SF small-plan filing with Schedule I focus

## Scenario

**Filer**: TechWidget LLC, a software services company

**Plan**: "TechWidget LLC 401(k) Plan" — established 2018, calendar plan year

**Filing year**: Plan year 2025

**Why this matters**: TechWidget has 50 active employees enrolled in the 401(k). Participant count is well under the 100-participant audit threshold. The plan invests entirely in mutual funds and money market accounts — all eligible plan assets. Filing is on Form 5500-SF (Short Form), no schedules attach, no IQPA audit required. Schedule I logic governs the financial summary even though 5500-SF is self-contained.

## Variant selection

Walked the decision tree:

1. SEP-IRA / SIMPLE IRA / governmental / church plan? No — qualified 401(k).
2. One-participant plan? No (50 active employees).
3. Welfare plan? No (pension plan).
4. Pension plan with < 100 participants? Yes (59 total).
5. Plan invests only in eligible plan assets? Yes (verified above).
6. → File **Form 5500-SF**.

## Filing channel

Form 5500-SF is filed **electronically only via EFAST2** — no paper option since 2010.

TechWidget's CFO has EFAST2 signer credentials (obtained when plan was established). The plan's TPA prepared the filing in IFILE; the CFO logs in, reviews, signs, and submits.

EFAST2 generates an Acknowledgment ID immediately. The filing is publicly searchable at https://www.efast.dol.gov/5500search within 24 hours.

## Schedule I logic note

Form 5500-SF is **self-contained** — no schedules attach. However, the financial-information sections of 5500-SF mirror the data that would appear on Schedule I of a full Form 5500 for a small plan that doesn't qualify for 5500-SF (e.g., a plan with non-eligible assets).

Schedule I differences (not relevant here since 5500-SF used):

- Schedule I would require a more detailed asset breakdown (cash, investments, receivables, etc.)
- Schedule I would require listing each large asset category separately
- Schedule I appears on full Form 5500 (not 5500-SF) — used by small plans that fail the eligible-asset test

For TechWidget's situation, 5500-SF is the right vehicle and Schedule I doesn't apply.
