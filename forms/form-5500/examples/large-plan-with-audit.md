# Example — 200-employee plan; full Form 5500 + Schedule H + IQPA audit

## Scenario

**Filer**: Acme Manufacturing Inc., a mid-size US manufacturer

**Plan**: "Acme Manufacturing Inc. 401(k) Profit-Sharing Plan" — established 2005, calendar plan year

**Filing year**: Plan year 2025

**Why this matters**: Acme has 200 active employees plus retirees and beneficiaries — total 218 participants on the first day of plan year 2025. The plan is **large** (≥ 100 participants), triggering full Form 5500 + Schedule H + an IQPA audit. The auditor's independence is a material concern; Acme's prior auditor was rejected by DOL because of a self-review threat.

## Variant selection

Walked the decision tree:

1. SEP-IRA / SIMPLE IRA / governmental / church plan? No — qualified 401(k).
2. One-participant plan? No (200 active).
3. Welfare plan? No (pension plan).
4. Pension plan with < 100 participants? No (218).
5. → File full **Form 5500** + Schedule H + IQPA audit + Schedule R.

---

## Auditor independence — a red flag

Acme's prior IQPA auditor was a regional firm that **also** prepared the company's tax return. DOL flagged this in Acme's 2023 examination as a violation of auditor independence under DOL Reg. 29 CFR §2520.103-1(b) and AICPA / GAAS independence standards. The same firm cannot:

- Audit the plan AND
- Prepare the plan's tax return / 5500 itself, OR
- Provide bookkeeping for the plan, OR
- Be the plan's actuary, OR
- Have a financial interest in the plan sponsor

Acme switched to **Smith & Jones LLP** for plan year 2024, an ERISA-specialty firm that audits the plan only. Smith & Jones does not provide other services to Acme. Independence preserved.

## Filing channel

Form 5500 + Schedule H + Schedule R + Schedule C + IQPA audit + asset schedules — **EFAST2 only**, electronic submission. Paper filing was discontinued for Form 5500 / 5500-SF in 2009.

The TPA (Fidelity, in this case acting as plan recordkeeper) typically prepares the 5500 in their software, generates EFAST2-compliant XML, and uploads to EFAST2. The plan administrator (Acme Benefits Committee Chair) reviews and signs with EFAST2 signer credentials.

Filing accepted; Acknowledgment ID saved.

## Auditor independence — what to verify

Each year, before signing the engagement, Acme's Benefits Committee verifies that Smith & Jones LLP:

1. Has no financial interest in Acme Manufacturing
2. Does not provide tax preparation, bookkeeping, plan administration, actuarial services, or investment management to the plan or sponsor
3. Has no Acme employees as partners or employees of the firm
4. Is registered with the AICPA Employee Benefit Plan Audit Quality Center
5. Is qualified under DOL Reg. 29 CFR §2520.103-1(b)

The committee documents this verification in the audit engagement file. DOL examiners may request the verification.
