# Excess APTC Repayment Limits (Pub 974 Table 5)

When advance Premium Tax Credit (APTC) exceeded the actual PTC the user qualified for, the excess is generally repaid via Form 8962 Line 29 → Schedule 2 Line 1a. But Pub 974 Table 5 caps the repayment for filers below 400% FPL.

The cap depends on:
1. Filing status (single vs. all others)
2. Household income as percentage of FPL (Form 8962 Line 5)

---

## 2025 Repayment Limits (use for tax year 2025 filing)

| Line 5 (% of FPL) | Single | All Other Filing Statuses |
|-------------------|--------|---------------------------|
| Less than 200% | $375 | $750 |
| At least 200% but less than 300% | $975 | $1,950 |
| At least 300% but less than 400% | $1,625 | $3,250 |
| 400% or more | NO CAP — full repayment | NO CAP — full repayment |

(Figures from IRS Publication 974 for tax year 2025; verify against the current Pub 974 for the year being filed.)

## How to apply

1. From Form 8962 Line 27 (Excess APTC) and Line 5 (Income % FPL):
2. Determine filing status: Single = single; all others (MFJ, MFS-with-exception, HoH, QSS) = "Other"
3. Look up the cap in the appropriate row
4. Form 8962 Line 28 = the cap
5. Form 8962 Line 29 = lesser of Line 27 or Line 28 → flows to Schedule 2 Line 1a

---

## ARPA repayment relief (one-time, tax year 2020 only)

The American Rescue Plan Act waived all excess APTC repayment for tax year 2020 only, in response to COVID-19 income volatility. This was a one-time provision and does NOT apply to 2021 or later years.

---

## Edge cases

### Case 1 — Income reported under 100% FPL
If actual household income came in below 100% FPL but APTC was paid based on a higher estimate, the user is generally not required to repay the excess (Pub 974, "Below 100% FPL" rules). Exception: if the user was ineligible for other reasons (e.g., dependent of someone else), full repayment applies.

### Case 2 — Income reported over 400% FPL
Under ARPA/IRA (2021–2025), no cap applies but PTC is still available — the cap doesn't matter because the user can still claim full PTC. Under pre-ARPA rules (possibly resuming 2026), no PTC eligibility above 400% FPL means full repayment of the entire APTC received.

### Case 3 — MFS with exception
A user who claims MFS with the domestic abuse / spousal abandonment exception uses the "All Other" column for repayment limits. The exception only affects PTC eligibility, not repayment cap calculation.
