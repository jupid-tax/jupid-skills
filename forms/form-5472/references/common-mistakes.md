# Top Form 5472 Mistakes

Real mistakes filers make on Form 5472, ranked by how often they trigger
$25,000+ penalties under IRC §6038A(d) or audit adjustments. Each
entry has the mistake, the consequence, and the fix.

## 1. Foreign-owned DE doesn't file at all

**The mistake**: Foreign individual forms a Delaware LLC (or Wyoming,
Nevada, etc.), uses it for e-commerce or holding, and never learns about
Form 5472. After 2-3 years, an IRS notice arrives.

**Why it happens**: Foreign owners are often advised by formation
agents (LegalZoom, ZenBusiness, IncFile, registered agent services)
that "an LLC has no US tax filing requirement if you have no US
income". This was true before 2017 but is wrong since Treas. Reg.
§301.7701-2(c)(2)(vi) took effect for tax years beginning January 1,
2017.

**Consequence**: $25,000 per year missed under IRC §6038A(d)(1). For a
DE that's been operating 4 years without filing, that's $100,000.
Penalties apply per related party — most foreign-owned DEs have one
foreign owner = one penalty per year.

**Fix**: File pro-forma 1120 + Form 5472 for every open year (typically
the 6 most recent years; the IRS can go back further if there's
fraud). Attach a reasonable cause statement to each year's filing
explaining the discovery. The IRS may abate some or all penalties if
the user acts promptly upon discovery.

---

## 2. Filing 5472 without pro-forma 1120 (Type 3)

**The mistake**: User learns about Form 5472 and files just the 5472,
without the pro-forma Form 1120.

**Why it happens**: The 5472 is the substantive form; the 1120 feels
unnecessary because the DE has no tax. Some users assume the IRS will
"figure out" the right form pairing.

**Consequence**: The IRS treats the 5472 as orphaned (no tax return to
attach to) and may consider it not validly filed. $25,000 penalty as
if no filing.

**Fix**: Always file pro-forma Form 1120 with attached Form 5472.
Notation "Foreign-owned U.S. DE" at top of 1120 page 1.

---

## 3. E-filing pro-forma 1120 + 5472 (doesn't work)

**The mistake**: User tries to e-file the pro-forma 1120 + 5472
through a tax software package.

**Why it happens**: Most tax software supports e-file. User assumes
all corporate returns are e-fileable.

**Consequence**: E-file rejection, or the software refuses to start.
Some software lets the user fill the 1120 but then errors at submission.

**Fix**: Type 3 DE filings are paper only. Print the package, sign,
fax to 855-887-7737 OR mail to the Ogden address (verify against
current Form 5472 instructions). Type 1 (US C-corp with 1120) and
Type 2 (foreign corp with 1120-F) ARE e-fileable through most major
tax software.

---

## 4. Missing the formation contribution in Year 1

**The mistake**: Foreign owner contributes $1,000 (or any amount) to
fund the LLC at formation. Year 1 Form 5472 reports $0 of
transactions. IRS sees the EIN was issued but no formation activity
reported.

**Why it happens**: Owners think "I just opened the bank account, I
didn't really 'transact'". But the capital contribution is a
reportable transaction for Form 5472 purposes.

**Consequence**: IRS notice questioning the $0 filing; possible
$25,000 penalty if the IRS deems the filing materially incomplete.

**Fix**: Always report the formation contribution in Part V of the
Year 1 Form 5472 as "Capital contribution received from foreign owner".
Include the amount and date.

---

## 5. Reporting $0 every year for a DE with actual activity

**The mistake**: DE has a US bank account that received and spent
funds during the year, but the foreign owner reports Form 5472 with
all-zero transactions.

**Why it happens**: Owners think "transactions with the related party"
means only direct transfers between owner and DE, not third-party
business activity.

**Consequence**: When the IRS audits and sees bank statements showing
material flows, they assume the 5472 is materially false. Penalties
escalate; civil fraud penalty (75%) possible.

**Fix**: Identify ALL related-party transactions:
- Capital contributions from owner (e.g., owner wired funds to LLC
  bank account to pay for inventory)
- Distributions to owner (e.g., owner withdrew profits)
- Services performed by owner for the DE without billing
- Loans between owner and DE
- Reimbursements
- Even small flows ($500 contribution) are reportable

If the only activity is third-party (e.g., the LLC sold goods to
unrelated US customers and used the funds to buy inventory from
unrelated suppliers), there may be no related-party transactions in a
given year — but every year has the formation contribution to report
in Year 1, and most years have at least some owner contribution or
distribution.

## 6. Wrong related party identified

**The mistake**: User has a complex ownership structure (e.g., DE is
owned by a foreign trust, which is owned by a foreign corporation).
They file 5472 listing the trust as the related party but not the
ultimate foreign individual.

**Why it happens**: The form asks for "25% foreign shareholder" and
the user picks the most immediate level.

**Consequence**: The IRS expects the **ultimate** 25%+ foreign owner
to be identified, plus all intermediate owners. Missing the ultimate
results in incomplete filing; missing intermediate is also incomplete.

**Fix**: For any chain of ownership, file separate Form 5472s for:
- The direct owner (Part II identifies them)
- Each intermediate (Part II identifies them in turn)
- The ultimate beneficial owner (Part II identifies the human
  individual at the top)

For each, Part III names the next level up (or "Same as Part II" if
direct).

---

## 7. Wrong filing channel (sent to state service center)

**The mistake**: User mails pro-forma 1120 + 5472 to the state IRS
service center where their personal 1040 normally goes.

**Why it happens**: 1040s and other returns go to state-specific
addresses; user assumes the same applies to Form 5472.

**Consequence**: The IRS may forward to Ogden internally, but the
timing slips. If the forwarded filing arrives at Ogden after April 15,
the IRS may treat as late and impose $25,000 penalty.

**Fix**: Pro-forma 1120 + 5472 goes to the specific Ogden address in
Form 5472 instructions, OR is faxed to the dedicated number
(855-887-7737, verify against current instructions). Do NOT send to
state service center; do NOT bundle with 1040.

---

## 8. Transactions reported net instead of gross

**The mistake**: US sub bought $5M of inventory from foreign parent
and sold $1M of inventory to foreign parent. User reports "$4M net" on
the inventory line.

**Consequence**: Form 5472 instructions explicitly require gross flows
in each direction. Reporting net misstates the line and may trigger
inquiry.

**Fix**: Report $1M in "amounts received from related party" column
and $5M in "amounts paid to related party" column on the inventory
sales line. Don't net.

---

## 9. Missing related party that's not a shareholder

**The mistake**: US sub of German parent has transactions with French
sister-corp (also owned by German parent). User files 5472 only for
the German parent, not for the French sister.

**Why it happens**: User thinks "5472 is for shareholders" and the
French sister isn't a shareholder of the US sub.

**Consequence**: $25,000 penalty for the missing 5472 covering the
French sister.

**Fix**: Form 5472 covers ALL related parties under §6038A, which
incorporates §267(b) related-party rules. Sister-corps under common
control (>50% owned by same person) are related under §267(b)(3) and
require their own 5472 if there were transactions.

---

## 10. Late filing without reasonable cause statement

**The mistake**: User realizes they missed the deadline and files
late without attaching a reasonable cause statement.

**Why it happens**: User hopes the IRS won't notice or won't penalize.

**Consequence**: IRS does notice (the filing is logged with date
stamp). $25,000 penalty assessed automatically. Without an
already-attached reasonable cause statement, the user has to
respond to the penalty notice within 30 days, slowing the
abatement process.

**Fix**: Attach a written reasonable cause statement to any late
filing. Cover:
- The original due date
- The actual filing date
- Specific facts and circumstances justifying the late filing
- Affirmation under penalty of perjury

The First-Time Abate program does NOT apply to §6038A penalties.
Reasonable cause is the only path.

---

## Less common but worth flagging

### 11. Combining multiple related parties on one 5472

Each related party gets its own Form 5472. Some users try to combine
"all related parties" into one form to reduce paperwork. Don't — the
IRS's processing matches related party to 5472 one-to-one.

### 12. EIN mismatch between Form 5472 and Form 1120

The DE has one EIN. Use it consistently on Form 1120 and on every
attached Form 5472 in Part I. Mismatched EINs trigger system rejection.

### 13. Treating a Stripe / Amazon Seller account as not a US presence

Foreign-owned DE selling on Amazon FBA or Stripe in the US. User
thinks "the LLC has no US office, so no US tax filing". The 5472
filing applies regardless of office presence; it's about ownership
structure, not US ECI.

Whether the DE has US ECI (and thus the foreign owner has 1040-NR or
1120-F filing duty) is a separate question. The 5472 filing duty is
universal for foreign-owned DEs.

### 14. Filing 5472 instead of 5471

Form 5471 reports foreign corporations owned by US persons (looking
DOWN the chain). Form 5472 reports US corporations owned by foreign
persons (looking UP the chain). They're not interchangeable. A US
person who owns a foreign sub files 5471, not 5472. A foreign person
who owns a US sub files 5472 (via the US sub), not 5471.

A US C-corp can have BOTH duties: 5471 for its foreign sub + 5472 for
its foreign parent. Both are filed separately.

### 15. Forgetting Form 5472 when LLC is owned by foreign trust

If a foreign trust owns 100% of a US LLC, the LLC is a foreign-owned US
DE under §301.7701-2(c)(2)(vi). The trust is the foreign owner. Form
5472 + pro-forma 1120 required, with the trust identified in Part II.

The trust separately may have Form 3520-A obligations if it has US
beneficiaries. Coordinate with the [`form-3520`](../../form-3520/) skill.

### 16. Calendar vs fiscal year confusion

A US C-corp can have a fiscal year. Form 5472 follows the C-corp's tax
year, not the calendar year. Foreign-owned DEs default to calendar year
unless an election was made on Form 1128.

### 17. Reporting amounts in foreign currency

Form 5472 amounts must be in USD. Translate per ASC 830 or per the
Treasury yearly average rate. Document the source. Reporting in EUR or
GBP is wrong even if the underlying transactions were in those
currencies.

### 18. Paying $25,000 penalty without disputing

User receives a $25,000 penalty notice and pays without responding.
Often the penalty is abateable with a reasonable cause statement, even
late. Always respond to the notice (within the response window) and
document why penalties shouldn't apply.

### 19. Reusing same reference ID for different related parties

The reference ID number on Line 2c is unique per related party. Reusing
the same ID for two different parties confuses IRS systems. Generate a
unique ID for each related party (often the foreign tax ID + a suffix,
or a sequential number).

### 20. Incomplete Part VII attestations

Part VII has Yes/No questions about the relationship and US tax
positions. Skipping these or marking everything "No" without thinking
triggers IRS review. Read each question against the user's facts and
answer truthfully.
